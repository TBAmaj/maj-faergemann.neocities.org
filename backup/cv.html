ARTIST STATEMENT

My practice explores the intersection of memory, space, and temporality through a multidisciplinary approach that includes painting, photography, installation, and digital media. I am interested in how personal and collective memories shape our understanding of place and identity.

Through layered compositions that combine abstraction and representation, I create visual palimpsests that speak to the fragmented nature of memory and experience. My work often incorporates found materials and archival imagery, creating a dialogue between past and present.

Recent projects have focused on urban environments and the traces of history embedded within architectural spaces. By documenting and reinterpreting these spaces, I aim to reveal the hidden narratives and emotional resonances that exist beneath the surface of everyday surroundings.
